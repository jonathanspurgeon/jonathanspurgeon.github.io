Progression Poetry: A poetic articulation of harmonic voyages

Last Updated: June 15, 2025

this is what IV-V-I feels like

The son departs his father's house,
Seeks fortune in a distant key—
Subdominant, he wanders far
From home's familiar melody.

In foreign lands his gold runs thin,
The dominant chord builds and swells,
Tension mounting, hunger growing,
Heart remembering what it knows.

At last the cadence calls him home,
That perfect fall from five to one—
The father's arms, the tonic's rest,
Resolution's work is done.
